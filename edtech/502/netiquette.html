Netiquette in the Online Course
Learner Description: This webpage is providing some rules for undergraduate students to follow during communicating online with instructors and peers.

Quick Tips for Learners:

  • Don't use online texting language in a class
  • Don't type in all caps
  • Use standard fonts
  • Watch your language
  • Avoid sarcasm
  • Help keep flame wars under control
  • Share expert knowledge

What is Netiquette?

The word netiquette is a combination of net and etiquette. It means respecting other usersâ€™ views and displaying common courtesy when posting your views to online discussion groups.

The Golden Rule.

Do unto others as you would have others do unto you. Imagine how you would feel if you were in the other person's position. Stand up for yourself, but try not to hurt people's feelings.

Use Appropriate Emotions.

Popular emotion icons such as :) or :( can be helpful to convey your tone, but do not overdo or overuse them.

Make Yourself Look Good Online.

You will be judged by the quality of your writing ,spelling and grammar. Be pleasant and polite. Don't use offensive language.

Be forgiving of mistakes.

If you do decide to inform someone of a mistake, point it out politely, and preferably by private email rather than in public and don't forget if it's a minor error, you may not need to say anything.
